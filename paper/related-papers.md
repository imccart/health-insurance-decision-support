# Related Papers on Insurance Intermediaries

## Directly competing

**Li, Liu, Lo Sasso & Tilipman — "Going for Broker? Intermediation in Health Insurance Markets"**
- Status: Preliminary, coming soon (no PDF/abstract as of March 2026)
- Authors at Georgetown (Tilipman), likely ACA setting
- Website: https://sites.google.com/view/anranli/home
- May be Medicaid setting (Li's other broker paper is Medicaid enrollment brokers, a different institution from ACA marketplace brokers). If so, not directly competing.
- Our differentiators if ACA: structural supply model (commission FOC), welfare decomposition (tau gradient), Covered California FOIA data with broker/navigator channel detail

## Related but different scope

**Karaca-Mandic, Feldman & Graven (2018) — "The Role of Agents and Brokers in the Market for Health Insurance"**
- Journal of Risk and Insurance, 85(1), 7-34
- Small employer market (not individual). Broker competition increases insurance offering and lowers premiums. Purely reduced-form, no structural model.
- https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jori.12139

**Gürel (2024) — "The Impact of Brokers on ACA Marketplace Growth"**
- Risk Management and Insurance Review, 27(2), 227-236
- ACA individual market, HealthSherpa data. Brokers drive enrollment growth, especially low-income and new enrollees. Descriptive, no causal identification or structural model.
- https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/rmir.12280

**Li, Zeng & Zhao (2025) — "The Impact of Intermediaries on Insurance Demand and Pricing"**
- Insurance: Mathematics and Economics, 122, 143-156
- Theoretical model comparing fee-based vs commission-based intermediary compensation. Commission systems create conflicts of interest. No empirical work.
- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0167668725000393
